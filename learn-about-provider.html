Learn About Providers

We know that knowing and understanding the differences between mental health providers can be overwhelming. So let us break it down for you.

Psychiatrist

This is a medical doctor who specializes in diagnosing and treating mental health disorders, typically using mostly medication and sometimes utilizing forms of psychotherapy. Psychiatrist visits are typically shorter in length and comprise evaluations, medication management, and treatment planning.

Psychologist

This is a mental health professional who specializes in the study of behavior and mental processes and provides therapy to help individuals manage mental health conditions. These individuals are skilled in assessment, diagnosis, psychotherapy, and also has a research background.

Licensed clinical social worker (LCSW)

This is professional who provides therapy and counseling services to individuals, couples, and families, with a focus on social and environmental factors that contribute to mental health issues. Social workers may also provide case management, and this group constitutes the largest sized group of mental health providers in the United States.

Licensed professional counselor (LPC)

This is a mental health professional who provides counseling services to individuals, couples, and families, helping them develop coping skills, problem-solving techniques, and strategies to manage emotional and behavioral issues.

Licensed marriage and family therapist (LMFT)

A mental health professional who specializes in treating relationship and family issues, and provides counseling services to couples and families.

Psychiatric nurse practitioner

A medical professional who is licensed to prescribe medication and provides mental health care services, including diagnosing and treating mental health disorders.

Certified peer specialist (CPS)

An individual who has personal experience with mental health challenges and has been trained to provide support, education, and advocacy to others with mental health conditions.

Art/Music Therapist

A mental health professional who uses art and/or music therapy techniques to help individuals explore and express their emotions, improve their mental health, and enhance their overall well-being.

Occupational Therapist

A health care professional who helps individuals with mental health conditions develop the skills needed to perform daily tasks and activities that are essential for their well-being.
